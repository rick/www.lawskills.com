CARTER et al. v. WHATLEY.
36975.
(97 Ga. App. 10)
(101 SE2d 899)
(1958)
Foreclosure, etc. Henry Superior Court. Before Judge Brown. October 19, 1957.
NICHOLS, Judge.
The verdict directed for the plaintiff was demanded by the evidence, and the trial court did not err in denying the defendants' motion for new trial.
Ed Whatley, trading as Spalding Gas Company, filed a foreclosure affidavit in the office of the Clerk of the Superior Court of Henry County, Georgia, on October 1, 1956, which recited in part the following: "Robert L. Carter and W. J. Carter, defendants herein, [are] indebted to said plaintiff in the sum of $536.40 principal and $78.00 as attorney fees for the collection of said debt, upon a contract retaining title, and who, on oaths, says that the original, or a true copy of same, is hereto attached and made a part hereof, and said indebtedness is past due." The title-retention contract referred to in the affidavit read in part as follows: "If purchaser fails to pay any instalment when due . . . seller may declare the unpaid balance immediately due and payable . . . Time is of the essence of this contract . . . Total cash price $480.84, total down payment $48.84, balance cash price $432.00, time price differential $104.40, total note amount (balance time price) $536.40 . . ."
On June 12, 1957, the levy under the above affidavit having been made on June 6, 1957, Robert L. Carter and W. J. Carter filed an affidavit of illegality to such foreclosure proceedings which read in part as follows: "1. On or about the 1st day of October, 1956, deponents did tender to plaintiff a cashier's check, on the 1st National Bank of McDonough, Georgia, in the sum of $536.40, which amount more than included what deponents are due to plaintiff; 2. Plaintiff is illegally attempting to collect from deponents interest at a rate greater than was provided in the contract sued upon or allowed by law--amounting to usury. 3. Plaintiff is illegally attempting to collect unearned interest before maturity. 4. Deponents have heretofore tendered the amount stated above, representing more than is due upon said contract, and now offer to pay into the court as follows: $432.00, principal, plus interest at 7% up to the time of said tender and this tender is made continuing and bona fide. 5. Said tender was made prior to the expiration of 10 days from the date demand was made upon defendants for attorney's fees the fi. fa. for attorney's fees being illegal. 6. Costs should be taxed against plaintiff for the reason that he refused the original tender."
On the trial the above affidavits were introduced without objection and the only other evidence adduced was the following stipulated facts: "Notice of intention to claim attorney's fees was mailed to defendants on July 30, 1956, by certified mail, and duly received by defendants on the 31st day of July, 1956, or on August 1st, 1956, and the alleged tender of $536.40 by cashier's check was made by W. J. Carter to counsel for plaintiff within 10 days from the receipt of said notice, to-wit: on August 9, 1956. A cashier's check, issued by The First National Bank of McDonough, Georgia, in the sum of $454.69 was deposited in the registry of the court at the time of the filing of the affidavit of illegality. Additional amounts, as the proofs might show are required, may be likewise deposited or in cash, without objection." At the close of the evidence the trial court directed a verdict for the plaintiff in amount of $536.40 plus $78 attorney's fees.
The defendants filed a motion for new trial on the general grounds which they later amended so as to assign error on the direction of the verdict. The amended motion was denied, and the defendants except.
In the case sub judice a verdict for the plaintiff was demanded inasmuch as the affidavit of illegality does not dispute the statements in the foreclosure affidavit that the debt is "past due and unpaid." Therefore, the only questions presented are whether the actions of the defendants, as set forth in the stipulated facts and the affidavit of illegality would authorize a finding that the plaintiff was not entitled to the attorney's fees as called for in the contract and the costs of court, and whether the contract sued on was usurious.
The contract set forth a balance cash price, a time price differential, and the total of these two items as the total note amount or balance time price. It also provided that in the event any payment was not made when due the "seller may declare the unpaid balance immediately due and payable," and that, "time is the essence of this contract."
The contention that the defendants were relieved of having to pay the attorney's fees and court costs because they had made a tender of the full time price within 10 days after the notice that the total time price was due is without merit.
The tender was made in the form of a check, and although the plaintiff would have been held to have waived this defect, if he objected to the tender on some other ground, where as here the plaintiff, according to the record, merely refused the check without giving a reason, which he of course had a right to do, it cannot be said that he waived the defect which rendered the tender ineffective. Code 20-1105; Holland v. Mutual Fertilizer Co., 8 Ga. App. 714 (70 S. E. 151); Kiser Co. v. Padrick, 30 Ga. App. 642 (14) (118 S. E. 791).
The contract provided that, in the event the vendee defaulted under the terms of the contract, the vendee would pay all costs of collection including reasonable attorney's fees. Where a contract provides for reasonable attorney's fees the amount of such fees is calculated under the provisions of the Act of 1953 (Ga. L. 1953, Jan.-Feb. Sess., pp. 545, 546; Code, Ann., 20-506). The attorney's fees awarded in the present case were awarded in conformity with such act.
Accordingly, the verdict directed for the plaintiff was demanded by the evidence and the trial court did not err in denying the defendants' motion for new trial for any reason assigned.
Judgment affirmed. Felton, C. J., and Quillian, J., concur.
Ray M. Tucker, contra.
Benjamin B. Garland, for plaintiffs in error.
DECIDED JANUARY 24, 1958.
