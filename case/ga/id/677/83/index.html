CLAY v. AETNA CASUALTY & SURETY COMPANY et al.
38442.
(102 Ga. App. 498)
(116 SE2d 686)
(1960)
Workmen's compensation. Floyd Superior Court. Before Judge Hicks. May 20, 1960.
NICHOLS, Judge.
The award of the State Board of Workmen's Compensation, based upon matters "on file" which do not appear in the certified copy of the "file" transmitted to the superior court under the provisions of Code 114-710, was not supported by the evidence.
Edward Clay, while an employee of Virginia Carolina Chemical Corporation, received a hack injury on April 25, 1957, and an agreement to pay compensation between the employee, the employer and the insurer (Aetna Casualty & Surety Corporation), was entered into, filed with the State Board of Workmen's Compensation and compensation paid under such agreement. The agreement provided for the payment of compensation until "terminated in accordance with the Workmen's Compensation Law." Thereafter, the claimant returned to work for the employer doing "light work" and was laid off a short time later together with other employees when the business of the employer slacked off, but was not rehired when business again picked up although new men were hired. The claimant requested a hearing and alleged in his request: "That he has been unable to reach an agreement with his employer in reference to his claim for compensation." On the hearing the employer's plant superintendent testified that the claimant was not rehired because he was not physically able to do the work. A letter from the company physician, who had treated the claimant, was read into evidence which letter stated that the claimant was unable to do heavy work. The claimant was a day laborer. The deputy director hearing the case found that there had been no change in condition, the claimant's physical condition, since he returned to work on June 27, 1957, that a "final settlement agreement" dated June 27, 1957, was executed by the claimant and filed with the board, and that therefore the claimant was not entitled to compensation The claimant first appealed to the full board and then to the Superior Court of Floyd County. The award of the deputy director was made the award of the full board and this award was affirmed by the superior court. The claimant now excepts to the adverse judgment of the superior court.
" 'The facts upon which the superior court was authorized to exercise jurisdiction were those, and only those, contained in the record transmitted to it by the Department of Industrial Relations.' Department v. Travelers Insurance Co., 177 Ga. 669, 672 (170 S. E. 883)." City of Hapeville v. Preston, 67 Ga. App. 350, 356 (20 S. E. 2d 202). If no final settlement receipt, agreement that no further disability existed as of such date, was executed by the claimant and approved by the board, then the hearing, instead of being one on a change in condition, must be construed to have been one authorized by Code 114-706 Where the burden is on the employer to show a change in the claimant's condition. See General Accident Fire &c. Assurance Corp. v. Teal, 100 Ga. App. 314 (111 S. E. 2d 113); National Surety Corp. v. Nelson, 99 Ga. App. 95 (107 S. E. 2d 718); and Brazier v. U. S. Fidelity &c. Co., 99 Ga. App. 588, 591 (109 S. E. 2d 309).
The question presented to the superior court was whether the award of the State Board of Workmen's Compensation was supported by the evidence, and the question before this court is whether the superior court correctly affirmed the award of the full board., It has been said in many cases that where the award of the State Board of Workmen's Compensation is authorized by the evidence neither the superior court nor this court has authority to reverse such award, no error of law appearing. The record, as presented to the superior court, must authorize the findings of the board. If such findings are not so authorized then such award must be reversed. Since the record as presented to the superior court did not contain any final settlement receipt the award denying compensation because the claimant did not "carry the burden" of showing a change in condition was not authorized, and the judgment affirming such award must be reversed.
This judgment is based on the record as presented to both the superior court and this court. The board is required to forward to the superior court "certified copies of all documents and papers then on file in their office in the matter." Code 114-710. While the deputy director, whose findings were adopted by the full board, stated that such final settlement receipts were in the file, such receipts do not appear, and it cannot be assumed that the file of the board is different from the file forwarded to the superior court under Code 114-710, supra.
Judgment reversed. Felton, C. J., and Bell, J., concur.
Matthews, Maddox, Walton & Smith, John W. Maddox, contra.
James Maddox, for plaintiff in error.
DECIDED SEPTEMBER 30, 1960.
