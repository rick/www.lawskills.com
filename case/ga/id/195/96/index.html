MORRIS, administrator, v. SHUMATE, executrix, et al.
17365.
(207 Ga. 652)
(63 SE2d 664)
(1951)
HEAD, Justice.
Equitable petition. Before Judge Guess. DeKalb Superior Court. October 31, 1950.
The petition failed to set forth a cause of action for any of the relief prayed, and the trial court properly sustained the general demurrer.
John M. Morris, as administrator of the estate of Roland G. Thomason, deceased, filed a petition against Ninnie A. Shumate and Vinnie Leel Cooper, as executrices of the estate of Mrs. Roland G. Thomason, and others, and in substance alleged: Roland G. Thomason, a widower, and the father of six named children, intermarried with Mrs. Tenie P. Purdue, a widow, in September, 1917, and resided with her in DeKalb County until the date of his death in January, 1930. Immediately after the marriage, Thomason and his wife told his children in the presence of each other, and in the presence of disinterested persons, that they had entered into a mutual covenant to the effect that, if Thomason died before his wife, she was to have the use and income of all of his property for and during her natural life, and that, thereafter, all of his property and all of her property, which she owned at her death, would vest in the children of Roland G. Thomason. The named children, who were all of the heirs of Thomason, relied fully and completely upon the expressed agreement by their father and stepmother. Prior to his marriage with their stepmother, Thomason had sold certain real estate and received therefor specified amounts of money, which he kept and had on hand at his death. Thomason had a pension sufficient to meet the needs of his wife and himself, and in addition, his wife collected $1000 insurance upon his life. After his death, the widow took possession of all of the money and personal property of the deceased, and thereafter collected the rents from his farm at the rate of $50 per year. Mrs. Thomason, immediately after the death of her husband, went to collect the rent of the farm and told the tenant that she and her deceased husband had agreed for her to collect the rent during the remainder of her life. She told the children of Thomason, and other disinterested persons, that she had kept everything intact in accordance with her agreement with her husband; and that, if they would just let everything alone as it was, when she died the children of Thomason would get everything they both had. The children of Thomason relied upon her promises and were lulled into inaction because they believed and trusted her, thought she was acting in good faith, and believed her to be an honorable and upright person. She did not ask for a year's support from the estate of her deceased husband, nor did she claim dower. She kept all of the money and all of his real estate and collected the rents from the farm of Thomason until the date of her death in August, 1948. Unknown to the children of Thomason, and shortly before her death, Mrs. Thomas on made a purported will, which has been probated in solemn form in the office of the ordinary. The will is a fraud, is void and voidable, and is inoperative, because it attempts to dispose of property the testatrix did not in truth own at the time of her death, but which she held subject to the agreement with her deceased husband, as a trust in favor of the heirs of Thomason. At all times after the death of Thomason, his widow, by her words and acts, professed ratification of the previous contract. The purported will made by her is tainted with fraud and deceit, is in violation of her agreement, and is a fraud practiced upon the heirs of Roland G. Thomason. She obtained possession of the money, personal property, and real property of Thomason, of the value of $35,000, or other large sums, by continually telling the children of her deceased husband that, under the agreement with their father, which she was keeping, they would take his and her property at the time of her death. A copy of the will is set out in the petition.
The prayers were: (1) for process; (2) that an accounting be had of the estate of Roland G. Thomason and his wife, and that the assets thereof be turned over to the petitioner, as administrator, to be distributed as directed by the court; (3) that the property of Thomason and Mrs. Thomason be decreed to be file property and assets of the estate of Roland G. Thomason in fee simple; (4) that the purported will of Mrs. Thomason be declared null and void, on the ground of fraud and deceit practiced upon the estate and children of Roland G. Thomason, deceased; (5) that the petitioner have such other and further relief as would be equitable and just; and (6) that an accounting be had.
A general demurrer to the petition was sustained, and the exception is to that judgment.
Moise, Post & Gardner, Bates Block, Smith, Kilpatrick, Cody, Rogers & McClatchey, Weekes & Candler, and J. A. McCurdy, for defendants.
Charles W. Anderson, Scott Lay Jr.,. and J. Milam Morris Jr., for plaintiff.
Judgment affirmed. All the Justices concur. FEBRUARY 14, 1951. REHEARING DENIED MARCH 14, 1951.
