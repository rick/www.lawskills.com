PARKER PLUMBING & HEATING COMPANY v. KURTZ.
24830.
(225 Ga. 31)
(165 SE2d 729)
(1968)
NICHOLS, Justice.
Certiorari was granted to the Court of Appeals in the present case to correct the holding that the agreement executed by the insured and set out in detail in the opinion of the Court of Appeals ( Kurtz v. Parker Plumbing & Heating Co., 118 Ga. App. 130 (162 SE2d 755)), was not an assignment of the cause of action.
The agreement which embodied more than a mere assignment of the cause of action was still an assignment of such cause of action, and the assignee must bring the action in its name. See Sullivan v. Curling, 149 Ga. 96, 101 (99 SE 533, ALR 124). Therefore, the Court of Appeals erred in reversing the grant of the summary judgment for the defendant where the undisputed proof showed that the plaintiff no longer had any interest in the cause of action.
Hurt, Hill, & Richardson, James C. Hill, Arnold Wright, Jr., for appellant.
ARGUED DECEMBER 10, 1968 -- DECIDED JANUARY 14, 1969.
