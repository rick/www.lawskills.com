WESTBROOK v. WESTBROOK.
39188.
(105 Ga. App. 67)
(123 SE2d 348)
(1961)
Action on bond, etc. Fulton Superior Court. Before Judge Alverson.
FELTON, Chief Judge.
The fact that a principal on a ne exeat bond goes beyond the jurisdiction of the court which has rendered a judgment against him in the case in connection with which the ne exeat bond is executed is a breach of the bond and it is not encumbent upon the plaintiff in a petition to forfeit such a bond to show that the principal has been absent from court when called upon to appear. Such a requirement would defeat the ends of justice when the principal is beyond the jurisdiction of the court and cannot be cited to appear.
This case arises out of an exception to the sustaining of a general demurrer to the application of Mary N. Westbrook, as amended, for the forfeiture of a ne exeat bond signed by Henry Westbrook as surety. The statement of the case by plaintiff in error, stated by the defendant in error to be substantially correct, is as follows: Plaintiff in error, hereinafter referred to as plaintiff, filed suit for divorce and alimony against Rudene Westbrook, as defendant. On February 17, 1960, an interlocutory order was entered awarding plaintiff temporary alimony and support for the minor children. On April 11, 1960, plaintiff filed application against defendant Rudene Westbrook to have him adjudged in contempt for failing to comply with said interlocutory order. On April 27, 1960, plaintiff filed application for the issuance of the writ of ne exeat which application together with the court's order thereon was served upon said defendant at Fulton County jail. The Clerk of Fulton Superior Court issued the writ of ne exeat on April 27, 1960, the bond having been fixed by the court's order at $250. On April 29, 1960, the defendant executed a ne exeat bond for said amount as principal, and the defendant in error joined in its execution as the security. Plaintiff's application against the defendant that he be adjudged in contempt of court came on to be heard on June 16, 1960, and by an order of that date he was so adjudged and given the privilege of purging himself on condition that he make smaller weekly payments of past defaults, that he continue making payments under the interlocutory order, and ordering the defendant into the custody of the sheriff to be released upon payment of $100 on past defaults. The defendant Rudene Westbrook was thereafter released upon payment of $100. Since the date of said defendant's release, he has failed to make any further payments and has failed to abide by any orders of the court respecting plaintiff's claims. On October 10, 1960, plaintiff filed her application against the defendant in error, Henry Westbrook, for the forfeiture of the defendant's ne exeat bond, the application having been made because the defendant, Rudene Westbrook, was neither making payments as required by the orders and adjudications of the court, nor was he available for personal service of process or arrest and incarceration under a certain criminal proceeding. The bond sought to be forfeited contained the condition that the defendant would be forthcoming to answer plaintiff's claim (or) shall abide by the order and decree of the court. To said application the defendant in error interposed his general demurrers. By an order dated March 15, 1961, the trial court sustained one ground of demurrer and granted plaintiff leave to amend her original application. In compliance with said order plaintiff filed her amendment, and thereafter the defendant in error filed his renewed general demurrer. By the order of the trial court, dated June 19, 1961, the court sustained the renewed general demurrer and ordered plaintiff's application dismissed, said order further adjudging that it was a final order on the sufficiency of the pleadings.
The condition of the bond is that: "if the said Rudene Westbrook, defendant, shall. be forthcoming to answer to the complainant's claim, or shall abide by the order and decree of the court, then this bond to be void; else of full force and virtue." The contention of the defendant in error is that, since the defendant, Rudene Westbrook, has not at any time failed to be and appear in person in court when so ordered, there has been no breach of the condition of the bond. This contention is erroneous for the reason that the conditions of such a bond are complied with when the principal is present at court or within its jurisdiction and subject to its process. Swain v. Jaudon, 147 Ga. 773 (95 SE 696). The petition upon which this proceeding is based alleges that the defendant, Rudene Westbrook, was not forthcoming to answer plaintiff's claim for alimony awarded her and has failed to abide by the contempt and purge order of June 16, 1960. As against a general demurrer this allegation is equivalent to an allegation that the principal on the bond had absented himself from the jurisdiction of the court. The fact that Rudene Westbrook has absented himself from the jurisdiction of the court is a breach of the bond under the foregoing authority. Otherwise the plaintiff would be helpless to enforce the bond because all a principal under such a bond would have to do to avoid the consequences of a breach thereof would be to put himself out of the reach of the processes of the court. The principal on such a bond could never be guilty of being absent from court when required to be present when he put it beyond the power of the court to demand his presence. The court erred in sustaining the general demurrer to the petition.
Judgment reversed. Bell and Hall, JJ., concur.
Bass, Burger, Kidd & Rich, Casper Rich, contra.
Ben F. Sweet, for plaintiff in error.
DECIDED DECEMBER 5, 1961.
