JACKSON, by Next Friend v. BOWEN.
43497.
(117 Ga. App. 761)
(161 SE2d 910)
(1968)
Action for damages. Chatham Superior Court. Before Judge Harrison.
FELTON, Chief Judge.
The judgment dismissing the petition for failure to redraft it was error, because there had been no ruling on the demurrers to Count 4, which had been added by a prior, timely amendment.
On February 21, 1966, Dorothy Loretta Jackson, by next friend, Dorothy Lee Jackson, brought an action against James W. Bowen for damages for personal injuries sustained as a result of falling from the defendant's truck because of his alleged negligence.
On June 14, 1966, the court overruled special demurrers to Paragraphs 16, 17, 19, 22, 29a and 29d of the petition and sustained a demurrer to Paragraph 14, allowing 15 days within which to amend same and withholding ruling on the general demurrer until after any amendment to Paragraph 14 should be filed. On June 28, 1966, the plaintiff amended her petition by designating it as Count 1, amending Paragraphs 19, 22 and 29, striking Paragraph 27, and adding counts 2 and 3 thereto. Count 2 was comprised of the incorporation of Paragraphs 1 through 6, 9 through 13, 15, 19 and 22 through 30 of Count 1, plus three additional paragraphs. On July 7, 1966, the defendant filed a general redemurrer to Count 1 and four demurrers to the petition as amended. On September 13, 1966, the court ruled as follows: "1. That the defendant's general redemurrer to the original petition of plaintiff, now designated as Count One thereof, be and the same is hereby sustained, inasmuch as defendant's original special demurrer to Paragraph 14 of said original petition was previously sustained and the plaintiff failed to amend said paragraph, and said count is dismissed. 2. That the general demurrers to Counts Two and Three of said petition be and the same are hereby denied. 3. That special demurrers 6, 7, 8 and 9 [which do not appear in the record], are sustained and plaintiff granted 10 days within which to amend. 4. That the consideration of special demurrer 10 [which does not appear in the record] is held in abeyance pending amendments to said petition to comply with the rulings herein. 5. That all other special demurrers are hereby overruled." On November 2, 1966, the defendant redemurred generally to Counts 2 and 3 of the petition as amended and moved to dismiss the petition on the further ground of plaintiff's failure to amend same within the time allowed by the court's September order. On November 7, 1966, the plaintiff 4 thereto and amended Count 2 by adding therein verbatim the allegations of Count 1 which had previously been incorporated into Count 2. On November 18, 1966, the defendant renewed his general and special demurrers to the petition and demurred to the petition as amended. On June 13, 1967, the court entered an order striking Count 3 and providing for the dismissal of the petition in 10 days unless the plaintiff redrafted same "in its entirety to set forth only the counts and allegations remaining in said petition, with the right of defendant to renew his demurrers to said redrafted petition." On June 16, 1967, the plaintiff filed a "redrafted petition," setting forth the allegations of Counts 2 and 4, as had been alleged by her previous amendments of June 28, 1966 and November 7, 1966. On June 23, 1967, the defendant again renewed his general and special demurrers to the petition, demurred to the petition as finally amended, and moved its dismissal on the ground of the plaintiff's failure to redraft it in accordance with the court's June 13, 1967 order. On December 12, 1967, the court sustained the defendant's motion to dismiss the petition.
The appeal is from the judgment of the court dismissing the petition. Also enumerated as errors are the judgments of the court sustaining the special demurrers to paragraphs numbered 14, 19 and 27 of Count 1; sustaining the general demurrer to, and dismissing, Count 1 for failure to amend same to meet the special demurrer to Paragraph 14; and requiring the redrafting of the petition.
There is no appeal from the judgment striking Count 3 of the petition. Regardless of the status of the pleadings in Counts 1 and 2 (which is virtually impossible to ascertain from the record under the circumstances), at the time the petition was dismissed on the ground of not having been redrafted as required by the court's order it contained a fourth count, which had already been added by a timely amendment prior to the court's order to redraft, and the demurrers to which had not been ruled on by the trial court. The judgment of the court dismissing the petition was error because it fails to rule on the issues raised by the demurrers to Count 4.
No ruling is made at this time as to the dismissal for failure to redraft as to counts other than Count 4. The failure to redraft as to counts other than Count 4 would not subject the whole petition to demurrer on the ground that the redrafting of the whole was improper.
WHITMAN, Judge, concurring specially.
I concur in the judgment of reversal and also specially on the grounds that the entire petition, including Count 4, was not subject to dismissal. It appears that Count 4 was included in the second amendment to plaintiff's petition of date November 7, 1966, and general and special demurrers were urged to Count 4 in a demurrer filed November 18, 1966. By order of June 13, 1967, without passing on the demurrers to Count 4, plaintiff was required to redraft her petition in its entirety. In the redrafted petition filed June 16, 1967, Count 4 was included. Renewed demurrers and motion to dismiss, filed June 23, 1967, included renewed and additional demurrers to Count 4, but these demurrers were not passed on and are still pending undisposed of. The order dismissing the entire petition has relation only to the requirement of the order of date June 13, 1967, which was predicated upon plaintiff's failure to amend Count 3 of her petition, with the right of the defendant to renew his demurrers to the redrafted petition. The headnote to the opinion correctly states the ruling of the court.
Joseph B. Bergen, for appellee.
Findley, Solms, Gannam, Head & Buchsbaum, James W. Head, for appellant.
