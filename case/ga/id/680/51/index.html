BRYAN et al. v. GRAVELY.
38022.
(101 Ga. App. 546)
(114 SE2d 442)
(1960)
Action on contract, etc. Floyd Superior Court. Before Judge Hicks. September 8, 1959.
BELL, Judge.
1. Whether a new count to a petition may be added by amendment is a matter properly raised by special demurrer.
2. The trial judge erred in sustaining the general demurrer to the count added by amendment for the reason that the count set forth a cause of action.
Richard Bryan and Bobby Lee Warren filed their petition in the Superior Court of Floyd County, against Allison A. Gravely, alleging that the plaintiffs and the defendant had entered into a written contract wherein the plaintiffs agreed to advance certain sums of money for the repair and renovation of designated houses owned by the defendant, and the defendant authorized and employed the plaintiffs to collect all the rentals on said houses and designated the manner in which the collected rentals should be allocated. The balance from such rentals collected was to be used in repayment of the money expended by the plaintiffs in repairing these rental properties. The plaintiffs made this contract a part of the petition. The plaintiffs further alleged that the defendant "is now attempting to cancel the rental agreements . . . and utterly refuses and fails to pay the amount now due plaintiff herein." A general demurrer was filed to the petition and this was sustained with leave to amend. The plaintiffs then amended their original petition, adding a second count, which, in so far as it is here relevant, in substance alleged: that at the request of the defendant and for his use and benefit the plaintiffs paid for repairs to buildings belonging to the defendant; that in consideration thereof the defendant promised to pay the plaintiffs the amount expended on these improvements on demand; and that this sum the defendant has refused to pay. The defendant filed a general demurrer to the second count which had been added by the amendment, and the trial court sustained the demurrer.
1. Where a petition is amended by adding a new count, the question as to whether the new count may be added by amendment is properly raised by special demurrer. It is error where a trial judge determines this question by a ruling on a general demurrer. Milton v. Milton, 195 Ga. 130 (23 S. E. 2d 411).
2. Where a count added by amendment to a petition contains allegations that the plaintiff, at the request of the defendant, and for the defendant's use and benefit, paid to persons named in an exhibit attached to the petition the sum of $2,160.91 for the repair of buildings, that the defendant promised to pay the sum so expended to the plaintiff on demand, and that the defendant had refused to pay, a cause of action based upon an alleged express oral contract was stated and the count was not subject to general demurrer. This is true even though the count added by amendment was subject to being stricken by special demurrer for the reason that it set forth a net and distinct cause of action. Neal & Son v. Stanley, 17 Ga. App. 502 (87 S. E. 718).
Wright, Rogers, Magruder & Hoyt, Robert G. Walther, for plaintiffs in error.
DECIDED APRIL 20, 1960.
