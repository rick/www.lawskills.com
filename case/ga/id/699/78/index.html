PIEL et al. v. W. W. LAWRENCE & COMPANY.
36079.
(93 Ga. App. 702)
(92 SE2d 617)
(1956)
Action on account. Before Judge Etheridge. Fulton Civil Court. December 23, 1955.
FELTON, C. J.
The court erred in sustaining the general demurrer to the defendant's cross-action and in dismissing it.
W. W. Lawrence & Company sued Piel Paint Company, a partnership composed of Erwin W. and Harvey Piel, on open account. The account attached to the petition contained charges and credits, including one credit of $327.63. Defendant denied the paragraphs of the petition and counterclaimed, alleging: "4. Further answering defendants show that defendants and plaintiff entered into an agreement in September, 1952, whereby plaintiff was to furnish a salesman one week a month for a period of a year for the purpose of contacting dealer, commercial and contract customers in an effort to promote and sell the paints of the plaintiff through the defendants, and to supply defendants with high quality paints. 5. Plaintiff breached said agreement in the following particulars: (a) The said salesman was furnished for only three weeks. (b) Paints furnished by the plaintiff were inferior and of poor and unsatisfactory quality resulting in loss of many valuable customers and great reduction in volume of business of defendants. 6. That as a result of plaintiff's breaching said agreement, defendants have been damaged in the amount of $10,000."
Plaintiff amended its petition by striking the allegations as to an open account, and in lieu thereof alleging as follows: "3. Petitioner shows that defendants are indebted to it in the sum of $1,292.07 plus interest from May 1, 1954, as will more fully appear from the facts set forth herein. 4. On or about December 1, 1953, defendants were indebted to your petitioner in the sum of $1,619.70 upon an open account as will appear by reference to the bill of particulars annexed hereto marked 'Exhibit A' and made a part hereof. 5. On or about December 1, 1953, petitioner acting through its agent, DeJongh Franklin, and defendants acting through their agent, duly authorized, Charles H. Bruce, entered into a compromise agreement, partly oral and partly in writing, whereby defendants agreed to return and petitioner agreed to accept all merchandise purchased by defendants from plaintiff and still on hand, and defendants agreed to pay your petitioner the balance remaining due on the account, after allowance by plaintiff of a credit amounting to the purchase price of the merchandise returned, to be paid in three equal installments on March 1, 1954, April 1, 1954, and May 1, 1954. It was agreed by the parties that this was in full settlement of all matters in dispute between them. 6. In accordance with the provisions of this agreement, plaintiff accepted said merchandise then on hand in return and issued a credit to defendants in the amount of $327.63 on December 31, 1953, leaving a balance remaining due on said indebtedness of $1,292.07. 7. Defendants have failed to make payments to your petitioner in the sum of $1,292.07 plus interest from May 1, 1954, under the terms and provisions of this agreement. 8. Although your petitioner has made demand for payment upon defendants, defendants have refused to pay said indebtedness."
There was no objection or demurrer to the amendment. The plaintiff demurred generally to the cross-action of the defendant. The court sustained the general demurrer to the cross-action and dismissed it. The judge, trying the case without a jury, found for the plaintiff the amount sued for. The defendant's motion for a new trial was denied, and it excepts to the order denying its motion, and to the judgment sustaining the plaintiff's demurrer to its cross-action.
The court erred in sustaining the general demurrer to the defendant's cross-action. The allegations with reference to the compromise settlement agreement were put in the pleadings by the plaintiff by amendment and stood automatically denied. The action by the trial court in ruling against the cross-action was premature. The cross-action was similar in nature to the main action. Both involved the breach of a contract. If the plaintiff had proved its allegations as to the compromise settlement, as to the making of the agreement, and as to the authority of the alleged agent of the defendant to make it, the defendant would not have been entitled to recover on its cross-action, because the settlement agreement would have covered it and the breach would have been settled by the agreement. If the plaintiff failed to prove the compromise agreement, or that anyone was authorized to make a final binding settlement for the defendant, it would have had a right to prove its recoverable damages resulting from the plaintiff's breach of contract, if a breach was shown.
The court erred in sustaining the demurrer to the cross-action and in dismissing it. The other proceedings were nugatory.
Judgment reversed. Quillian and Nichols, JJ., concur.
Lipshutz, Macey & Franklin, contra.
Walter V. Beasley, for plaintiff in error.
DECIDED APRIL 4, 1956.
