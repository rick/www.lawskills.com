FLAHERTY et al v. DILLON.
17807.
(209 Ga. 25)
(70 SE2d 377)
(1952)
Equitable petition. Before B. B. Heery, Judge pro hac vice. January 15, 1952.
WYATT, Justice.
It was not error to sustain the demurrer and dismiss the petition in this case.
Edmund B. Flaherty, John W. Flaherty, Joseph J. Flaherty, Bernard M. Flaherty, Mary K. Flaherty, Thomas Flaherty, and Ray Flaherty filed a suit in equity against Annie D. Dillon, in which it was alleged in substance: that Mary Agnes Flaherty died January 11, 1925, leaving a will, and that Thomas Edmund Flaherty, the father of the petitioners, was duly appointed executor; that he duly secured an order to sell and did sell certain real estate, the property of the deceased, for $22,500, the sale being consummated August 10, 1929; that, at about that time, the health of Thomas Edmund Flaherty failed and he moved to Asheville, North Carolina, and that he "entrusted all of his affairs, including those as executor of the estate of the said Mary Agnes Flaherty, in the care of the said Annie D. Dillon, who was given power to collect, assume charge of, and also to distribute the assets of the said estate"; that Annie D. Dillon is disposing of her property preparatory to leaving the jurisdiction of the courts of this State; and that Kate Flaherty, the mother of the petitioners, died August 31, 1946.
A copy of the will of Mary Agnes Flaherty was attached, showing that a trust estate was provided for with Thomas Edmund Flaherty named as executor, the income from the property of the deceased to be used for the support of the executor and his family during his life, and at his death, for the joint support of his wife and children, with the support of each child terminating when the child reaches the age of twenty-one, and when the youngest child reaches the age of twenty-one, the entire property of the estate to be sold and the proceeds equally divided among the children of Thomas Edmund Flaherty. Upon the death of Thomas Edmund Flaherty, the will nominated his widow to succeed him as executrix, and to succeed her, the oldest child. It was alleged that the petitioners had, since the death of their mother, demanded an accounting and settlement on the part of Annie D. Dillon. The prayers were for an accounting, an injunction, and general relief.
The trial judge sustained a general demurrer to the petition, which was dismissed. The exception here is to that judgment.
We recognize the well-established rule to the effect that, under certain circumstances, the courts of ordinary and equity courts have concurrent jurisdiction over the settlement of accounts of administrators and executors. Code, 113-2203. It is also true that this court has said generally that, when a petition for accounting sets out facts indicating that something would be found to be due the petitioners, the petition is good as against a general demurrer. Southern Feed Stores v. Sanders, 193 Ga. 884 (20 S. E. 2d, 413).
The case here presented is not, an application of these rules of law, but is whether or not these plaintiffs can maintain this suit in any event. The petition in the instant case shows that the estate of Mary Agnes Flaherty is now unrepresented, no one having qualified as executor or executrix. The petition alleges that mortgages and liens exist as against the property of the deceased, which negatives the idea that no administration of the estate is necessary. The effect of the petition is to seek the recovery of property, largely personal, belonging to the deceased in the hands of a person who is not a legal representative of the estate of the deceased, the petition having been filed by the heirs at law of the deceased. This court has repeatedly held that this can not be done under the circumstances appearing in this petition. Carr v. Berry, 116 Ga. 372 (42 S. E. 726); Murphy v. Pound, 12 Ga. 278; Smith v. Smith, 141 Ga. 629 (81 S. E. 895). It follows, the judgment sustaining the general demurrer and dismissing the petition was not error.
Judgment affirmed. All the Justices concur.
Arthur L. Purvis and Myrick & Myrick, for defendant.
Harry P. Anestos, for plaintiffs.
