PRATHER v. MOORE.
36347.
(94 Ga. App. 541)
(95 SE2d 398)
(1956)
Action on note. Before Judge Manning. Cobb Superior Court. May 11, 1956.
GARDNER, P. J.
The court did not err in denying the motion for new trial.
This suit arose on a petition filed by Hardie Moore, trading as Canton Motor Sales, against T. E. Prather. The petition alleges substantially that the defendant signed a promissory note payable to the plaintiff in the amount of $800. The record shows that at the time of the signing of the note the plaintiff sold a truck to the defendant. The note was made payable 90 days from the date of the sale. Approximately 30 days after the note became due, the defendant sold to the plaintiff a power saw and the plaintiff gave credit in amount of $100 on the note, leaving a balance of $700 due on the note, besides interest. Immediately after this last transaction between the parties the plaintiff gave notice to the defendant that after the expiration of ten days, unless the balance of the note was paid, the plaintiff would institute suit against the defendant for $700 balance principal, besides interest and besides attorney's fees. The defendant did not pay anything further on the note and the plaintiff entered suit against the defendant for the principal, interest, and attorney's fees. Before pleading to the merits, the defendant filed demurrers, both general and special, to the petition. The plaintiff amended his petition in open court by striking from the petition any claim for attorney's fees. Thereupon the court overruled the demurrers. The defendant answered, admitting the execution of the note but denying any liability thereon, for the reason that the defendant had paid the note in full and did not owe the plaintiff any amount whatsoever. The case proceeded to trial before a jury; both parties introduced evidence. The court charged the jury. The jury returned a verdict against the defendant for the principal amount of $700 plus interest thereon. The defendant filed a motion for new trial on the statutory grounds and thereafter added ten special grounds. The court denied the motion. It is on this judgment that the defendant assigns error here.
1. The only issue of fact submitted to the jury was whether or not the defendant had paid the note. As to this there was a sharp conflict in the evidence. The jury resolved this issue against the defendant. There was sufficient evidence to sustain the verdict as to the general grounds.
2. The record reveals that the defendant admitted the execution of the note for which suit was brought and claimed that he had fully paid it by returning the truck to the plaintiff in full satisfaction of the note. The evidence on this point is conflicting, but the jury saw fit to believe the evidence produced by the plaintiff.
It is our opinion that nine of the ten special grounds of the amended motion are incomplete and present no issuable contention sufficient to authorize this court to pass thereon. The one exception is special ground 9 which assigns error in that the court erroneously charged regarding attorney's fees. The court in its order denying the motion for new trial struck from the original judgment the recovery of attorney's fees. Therefore this question is not here for determination since the defendant filed no exceptions to the order of the court striking the item for recovery of attorney's fees. All other contentions of the defendant have no merit and do not require a reversal.
Judgment affirmed. Townsend and Carlisle, JJ., concur.
A. J. Henderson, A. J. Henderson, Jr., Marion T. Pope, Jr., contra.
Claud M. Hicks, J. Douglas Henderson, for plaintiff in error.
DECIDED NOVEMBER 9, 1956.
