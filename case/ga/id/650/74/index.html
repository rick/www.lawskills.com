GULF OIL CORPORATION v. SIMS.
41364.
(112 Ga. App. 68)
(143 SE2d 766)
(1965)
Service, etc. McDuffie Superior Court. Before Judge Stevens.
HALL, Judge.
A petition for an order to perfect service of a petition, brought more than 5 years after the original action and when twenty-two terms of court have elapsed, must show that the plaintiff has taken diligent action since bringing the action in an effort to perfect servIce.
In this case the plaintiff assigns error on the judgment of the trial court sustaining the defendant's general demurrer to the plaintiff's petition, which prayed that the defendant be served with a petition and process, filed and dated April 11, 1959, that had been lost, with a "Judgment Establishing Lost Office Papers," and dismissing the action.
The plaintiff's petition for service and the record show these facts: The original action on an account was filed by an attorney now deceased and was docketed by the clerk of the superior court. There was no service nor purported service on the defendant. The case was marked in default at the September term 1959, and a verdict was found and a judgment entered for the plaintiff. An execution was issued and recorded in the general execution docket. In December 1962, the plaintiff filed a garnishment proceeding based on its judgment against the defendant and recovered a judgment against the garnishee. In January 1963, the defendant filed an equitable action to set aside the judgment against him for want of service of the petition upon him; in September 1964, there was a verdict in favor of the defendant (plaintiff in that action.) The original petition and process in the account action is missing from the records of the office of the clerk of the superior court, and in October 1964, on application of the plaintiff, a copy was established in lieu of the lost originals. The trial court heard the petition ex parte and, reciting that the facts set forth therein were found to be true, on October 23, 1964, ordered that service of the established lost petition and process be perfected upon the defendant.
The issue presented by the defendant's general demurrer to the petition in this case is whether the facts alleged, and recited above, show diligence by the plaintiff upon which the court could exercise its discretion to order service perfected. The petition shows that the original petition was filed but was not served on the defendant. No return of service was entered on the petition and process. (The plaintiff's petition to establish lost office papers alleges, "An exact copy of said lost office papers including all entries thereon, is hereto attached." (Emphasis supplied). The papers attached to that petition were a petition alleging indebtedness on an account with a statement and verification of the account attached, with the notation "Filed in office this 11th day of April 1959. /s/ Allen Pannell, Clerk," and a process signed by the clerk dated April 11, 1959). The plaintiff took a default judgment against the defendant. More than five years after filing the petition the plaintiff brought its petition to perfect service. During this time there had been twenty-two terms of court.
The specific facts alleged in the present cafe do not support the allegation that the "petitioner has exercised all diligence in an effort to have service of the petition and process perfected upon defendant." This general allegation, therefore, does not cause the petition to show that the court had discretion to order service perfected. Green v. Spears, 181 Ga. 486, 490 (182 SE 913); American Surety Co. v. Groover, 64 Ga. App. 865, 867 (14 SE2d 149); 71 CJS 87, 32; Anno. 21 ALR2d 929. The plaintiff relies on McClendon v. Ward-Truitt Co., supra. There the petition alleged that the plaintiff had exercised all diligence in having service perfected, and the court held that the facts set forth in that petition, including the fact that the sheriff had made an entry that he had served the defendant personally (which fact is absent in the present case) supported the allegation of diligence. Decisions to the effect that a general allegation of negligence is sufficient against a general demurrer have been made upon pleadings that contain an allegation of some act or omission which could be found to be negligence in fact (see McPhail v. Atlantic C. L. R. Co., 93 Ga. App. 599, 601 (92 SE2d 558), and are not controlling in this case.
The facts alleged did not authorize the court on October 23, 1964, to grant the plaintiff's petition for an order to perfect service on the defendant. The trial court did not err, therefore, in sustaining the defendant's general demurrer and dismissing the action.
Judgment affirmed. Bell, P. J., and Frankum, J., concur.
Kenneth Goolsby, Randall Evans, Jr., contra.
Robert E. Knox, Warren D. Evans, for plaintiff in error.
