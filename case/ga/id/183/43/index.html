LOCKE v. GRIMES.
18863.
(211 Ga. 447)
(86 SE2d 303)
(1955)
Habeas corpus. Before Judge Hicks, presiding. Laurens Superior Court. November 29, 1954.
ALMAND, Justice.
In a habeas corpus proceeding for the custody of minor children, between their father and maternal grandfather, the evidence demanding a finding that the father had not lost or forfeited the right to control and custody under Code 74-108 et seq., the error in admitting in evidence a report of the county welfare office as to the fitness of the father was harmless, and presents no cause for setting aside the order of the court awarding custody of the children to the father.
Jessie L. Grimes brought a habeas corpus proceeding against James W. Locke, seeking award of the custody of his three minor children, alleged to be illegally detained by the respondent. The respondent, the maternal grandfather of the children, their mother being dead, filed a response, in which he alleged that the petitioner on March 10, 1952, in consideration that the respondent would supply the care and attention of a father of the children and educate them, had surrendered the custody of the children to him; and further alleged that the petitioner has lost his parental control by reason of his abandonment of the children and his failure to provide them with the necessaries of life. On the hearing of the application, the court, over objections of the respondent, admitted in evidence a copy of a report made by the Laurens County Department of Public Welfare to the Judge of Laurens Superior Court, as to the fitness and ability of the petitioner to have the custody of the children. The admission of this report was objected to on the ground that it was not verified by any person and was not admissible in evidence, being merely a report of the welfare board based upon information and matters obtained out of court. Error is assigned in the bill of exceptions on the admission of this report in evidence. After hearing evidence, the court passed an order awarding the custody of the children to the petitioner. Error is likewise assigned on this order.
The bill of exceptions does not contain any evidence other than the welfare report, though the record does show that other evidence was introduced.
2d 874), such error was harmless, for the reason that, there being no evidence to show that the father had lost his parental control or right of custody, the question of fitness as between the parties was not at issue; and since this report dealt only with the question of the fitness of the father, its admission did not in any way work injury to the respondent.
Under the facts and circumstances of this case, it was not error to award the custody of the children to the father.
Judgment affirmed. All the Justices concur.
W. O. Purser, contra.
B. B. Hayes, E. L. Stephens, Jr., for plaintiff in error.
