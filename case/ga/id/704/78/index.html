ORR v. LYONS et al.
35548.
(91 Ga. App. 833)
(87 SE2d 225)
(1955)
Complaint. Before Judge Hendrix. Fulton Superior Court. November 24, 1954.
NICHOLS, J.
1. Code 81-501 deals with jurisdiction. Accordingly, the plaintiff's ground of demurrer, that the traverse was not in compliance with the above-mentioned section is without merit, since the traverse had reference to lack of legal service upon the defendant Lyons.
2. The ground of demurrer, that the traverse was void because it failed to show the defendant Lyons' actual present residence, is without merit for the reason above mentioned.
3. The ground of the plaintiff's demurrer, that the traverse and the court's order thereon were void because the order directs a return to the next term of court, is without merit for the reason that a demurrer may not be used to attack an order of court, and as ruled above, the traverse is not subject to the objection mentioned in headnote 1.
4. The provisions of Rule 26 of the Superior Courts (Code, Ann. Supp., 24-3326) apply only to service on the attorney of record after the defendant has been properly served and brought into court, and has employed an attorney to represent him. Harris v. Harris, 205 Ga. 105 (1) (52 S. E. 2d 598); Beebe v. Smith, 76 Ga. App. 391, 402 (46 S. E. 2d 212). In the present case there was no attorney of record on whom service could be perfected. Accordingly, the trial court did not err in sustaining Lyons' demurrer (without waiving the traverse) to the profferred amendment, in which the plaintiff sought to perfect service by serving the attorney who was not in fact the attorney of record for Lyons.
5. Where, as recited in the court's order, dismissing the action as to Lyons, it was stipulated that the facts alleged in the traverse were true, the court did not err, after having theretofore overruled the plaintiff's demurrer to the traverse, and having sustained the demurrer to the plaintiff's proffered amendment seeking to perfect service upon an attorney who was not Lyons' attorney of record, in dismissing the action as to Lyons.
Mrs. Harrison B. Orr brought an action against Jack Lyons and John Morrell & Company as codefendants, for damages resulting from an automobile accident, which occurred on April 5, 1952, and in which the plaintiff's husband was injured. John Morrell & Company was served by leaving process with one of its agents. Jack Lyons was alleged to have been served by a copy of the process being left at his most notorious place of abode in Fulton County. A traverse was filed to the service on Lyons by an attorney employed by an insurance company to represent him in any action arising out of the aforesaid automobile accident. The traverse alleged the following: On the day of the accident Lyons was living at the address in Atlanta, Georgia, where service was attempted to be made on him. Later in the year 1952 he moved from that address in Atlanta, Georgia, to parts unknown and his whereabouts was unknown at the time when the traverse was filed and has not been known at any time since he moved. Service has never been had on Lyons, nor has he appeared in court or otherwise waived service in his action. The plaintiff filed a demurrer to the traverse, in which she alleged that the traverse set forth no defense to the action; that the traverse was filed by Lyons' attorney in contravention of Code 81-501; that, as required by Code 81-502, it fails to set forth where in Georgia the defendant Lyons would be liable for suit; and that the traverse and the judge's order thereon are void because they require the plaintiff and others to show cause at the next term of court as to why the traverse should not be sustained. The plaintiff then filed an amendment to the original petition, seeking to serve Lyons by serving, as his attorney of record, the attorney who filed the above-mentioned traverse. The attorney, without waiving his traverse, filed a demurrer to this amendment in the name of the defendant Lyons. The plaintiff's demurrer to the traverse was overruled, and Lyons' demurrer to the plaintiff's amendment to her petition was sustained. When the traverse came on for hearing, the parties stipulated that the facts alleged in the traverse were true. The trial judge sustained the traverse and dismissed the petition as to the defendant Lyons. The plaintiff excepted to the order overruling her demurrer to the traverse, sustaining Lyons' demurrer to the amendment to her petition, and sustaining his traverse of service, thereby dismissing the action as to the defendant Lyons.
W. Colquitt Carter, Alex W. Smith, Jr., contra.
Stow & Andrews, Head & Hanna, for plaintiff in error.
DECIDED APRIL 11, 1955.
