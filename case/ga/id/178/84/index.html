HARTLEY v. HARTLEY.
19138.
(212 Ga. 62)
(90 SE2d 555)
(1955)
HEAD, Justice.
Motion to tax costs. Before Judge Humphrey. Washington Superior Court. August 23, 1955.
When the remittitur on the former appearance of this case ( Hartley v. Hartley, 211 Ga. 616, 87 S. E. 2d 851) reached the trial court, counsel for the plaintiff in error made an oral motion to tax "two-thirds" of the total costs on appeal against the defendant in error. This motion was denied, and the trial judge taxed all of the costs on appeal against the plaintiff in error. The trial judge denied an oral motion to require the clerk of the superior court to reenter, instanter, the justice court execution under attack in the former case. The plaintiff in error excepts to both rulings. Held:
2. The judgment of this court in the former case having been made the judgment of the trial court, no further order is required with reference to the justice court execution for the protection of the rights of the plaintiff in error. "The remittitur, whenever presented, is the proper evidence to the court below of the decision of this court; and the decision thus evidenced is to be respected and in good faith carried into effect. Code, 4285 [now 6-1804]. To do this is not discretionary with the presiding judge, but matter of statutory mandate and compulsion." Goldsmith v. Georgia Railroad Co., 62 Ga. 542, 544.
T. A. Hutcheson, contra.
Casey Thigpen, for plaintiff in error.
SUBMITTED OCTOBER 10, 1955 -- DECIDED NOVEMBER 14, 1955.
