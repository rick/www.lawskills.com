MORRIS v. OWEN et al.
38330.
(102 Ga. App. 71)
(115 SE2d 604)
(1960)
Action for damages. Gordon Superior Court. Before Judge Davis. March 14, 1960.
GARDNER, Presiding Judge.
The trial court erred in sustaining the general demurrers and dismissing the petition.
This is a suit for damages brought by Jack Morris against W. V. Owen and J. C. Owen, d/b/a Owen Drug Company. The record slows that no service was ever perfected on J. C. Owen, thus leaving W. V. Owen, d/b/a Owen Drug Company, as the defendant. To the amended petition the defendant filed general and special demurrers. The court sustained the general demurrers, not passing on the special demurrers. It is to the sustaining of the general demurrers that the case is here for review.
The petition alleges substantially as follows: (2) That Mrs. Alice Thelma Morris was and is the wife of the plaintiff; (3) that the plaintiff's wife was, on or about June 1, 1957, under the care and treatment of Dr. W. D. Hall, a practicing physician in Gordon County, Georgia; (4) that Dr. Hall gave the plaintiff's wife a prescription for drugs, which prescription was filled at the drug store operated by the defendant; (5) that the prescription provided for RX-Elixir Bromide and Chloral Compound 180.0; (6) that the plaintiff's wife used the original prescription from time to time thereafter until about November 7, 1958, the defendant having refilled the prescription without the knowledge or consent of the petitioner until November 7, 1958, and that the plaintiff's wife became addicted to the use of the drugs so that she could not resist the desire for them; (7) that the prescription was refilled by the defendant without the prescription being renewed; (8) that Chloral Compound is habit forming being so designated by Title 21 352 Subsection (d) of the United States Code Annotated and that that law provides that the drug shall "be dispensed only (i) upon a written prescription of a practitioner licensed by law to administer such drug or (ii) upon an oral prescription of such practitioner which is reduced promptly to writing and filed by the pharmacist, or (iii) by refilling any such written or oral prescription if such refilling is authorized by the prescriber either in the original prescription or by oral order which is reduced promptly to writing and filed by the pharmacist"; (9) that the sale or dispensing of any drug containing chloral is prohibited under the provisions of Code 42-807 except upon the written prescription of a physician and neither a written nor an oral prescription shall be refilled, but a new prescription shall be written and filed; (10) that in refilling the prescription the defendant violated the provisions of the Federal and State laws; (11) that because of the habit forming qualities of the drug the plaintiff's wife could not resist refilling the prescription and taking same when it was refilled; (12) that as a result of consuming large quantities of the prescription the plaintiff alleges that his wife became nervous and lapsed into a semi-comatose condition and became so mentally confused that when she was placed in a chair it was necessary to physically restrain her; (13) that because of her mental condition on or about November 7, 1958, she was adjudicated an incompetent by the Court of Ordinary of Gordon County and as a result she was admitted to Milledgeville State Hospital on that same date and began a treatment for bromide poisoning of which she was cleared on December 10, 1958, and was subsequently discharged from the Milledgeville State Hospital; (14) that the said condition referred to was caused by excessive use of the prescription; (15) that before she was admitted to the hospital she suffered from hallucinations and after having consumed large doses of the prescription she immediately became confused; (16) that the condition of the plaintiff's wife was proximately caused by the acts of the defendant in refilling the prescription; (17) that prior to the time of which complaint is made herein the plaintiff's wife carried on the usual household duties of a wife; (18) that because of her having taken the prescription herein referred to the plaintiff lost consortium and companionship of
his wife; (19) that the act of the defendant in refilling the prescription without the written or oral prescription of Dr. Hall constituted a violation of the State and Federal laws to which reference is made hereinabove; (20) that because of the facts set out in this petition the plaintiff was damaged in the sum of $10,000, for which suit is brought.
The druggist knew, from the allegations of this petition, that the drug was habit forming; he knew that the sale of such drug without a renewal prescription was a violation of a Federal statute; he knew that the plaintiff's wife was continuing to purchase the drug without such renewal prescription, and he further knew that the drug was for her own use, since it was sold pursuant to the original prescription made out by the physician for her use.
Under Code 84-1313, a pharmacist in this State is required to be a graduate of a recognized college of pharmacy and also to have had twelve months of practical experience before being licensed. It is accordingly not necessary to allege that a licensed pharmacist would know the effects of habit-forming drugs. The petition states that the plaintiff's wife had to be committed to Milledgeville State Hospital as a result of the addiction, and damages to the husband were properly alleged. The petition does not allege that the husband knew of his wife's continued use of the drug.
The trial court erred in sustaining the general demurrers and dismissing the petition.
Judgment reversed. Townsend, Carlisle and Frankum, JJ., concur.
Harry T. Lawrence, contra.
James Maddox, for plaintiff in error.
DECIDED JULY 11, 1960.
