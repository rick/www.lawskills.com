MAXWELL et al. v. SUMMERVILLE LUMBER CO.
34355.
(87 Ga. App. 405)
(74 SE2d 111)
(1953)
Materialman's lien, etc.; from Chattooga Superior Court-- Judge McClure. September 20, 1952.
GARDNER, P. J.
Suit may be brought in a proper case for the recovery of a debt for materials furnished and also for the foreclosure of a materialman's lien against the property improved, and in such a case the plaintiff may pray for a general judgment against the defendants and also pray that such judgment be declared a special lien against the improved realty described in the petition.
Summerville Lumber Company Inc. brought suit in the Superior Court of Chattooga County against Fred Maxwell and Doris Maxwell, and alleged that the defendants had obligated themselves to it for furnishing lumber to them for the building of a building on certain property owned by them, in the sum of $500.75. The petition alleged that the materials had been furnished and used in a building on described realty in that county; and that the plaintiff lead performed its contract, and there was a balance due it of $500.75, for which demand had been made of the defendants and refused. The plaintiff set up that, within three months from the completion of the contract, it recorded its claim of lien upon the building aid realty, as required by law, in the office of the clerk of the court; and prayed for a general judgment against the defendants for the amount due and also for a special judgment against the property described in the petition, "giving said petitioner a lien upon said real estate." The defendants denied liability. The case was tried, and a jury returned this verdict: "We, the jury, find for the plaintiff in the sum of $500.75 principal debt, $43.26 interest, and $21.25 costs of suit. We further find that the plaintiff's heir as prayed for be set up and established upon the property described in the petition." The court accordingly entered up this judgment: "Whereupon, it is considered, ordered and adjudged by the court that the plaintiff Summerville Lumber Company Inc., do have and recover of the defendants, Fred Maxwell and Doris Maxwell, the sum of $500.75 principal debt, $43.26 interest, and $21.25 costs of suit. It is further considered, ordered and adjudged by the court that the plaintiff's lien, as a materialman's lien, to the amount of said principal, interest and costs, be set up and established upon the following property: [Describing the property described in the petition]."
Thereupon an execution was issued by the clerk of said court and duly levied on the property of Doris Maxwell, owned individually, and not on the property improved and owned jointly by Fred Maxwell and Doris Maxwell. The defendants filed their motion in said superior court for an order making the judgment in the case conform to the verdict rendered by the jury, the said verdict being a special finding in favor of the lien, and the judgment entered up by the court being a general judgment. On September 20, 1952, the superior court dismissed this motion, and to this judgment the defendants except in the present bill of exceptions. The court then allowed the levy to proceed upon the individual property of Doris Maxwell, and the defendants assign the same as error. No demurrers were filed to the petition.
The verdict of the jury in this case was a general verdict and a special verdict, and not a special verdict or one finding in favor of the lien only, and the judgment entered properly followed the verdict. The plaintiff prayed for a general judgment against the defendants for $500.75, and also for a special judgment against the property improved, "giving said petitioner a lien upon said real estate." The verdict was a general one finding for the plaintiff for the full amount sued for, and was a finding "that the plaintiff's lien as prayed for be set up and established upon the property described in the petition." This was not a verdict finding in favor of the lien only, as contended by the defendants in their motion to conform the judgment to the verdict, and in their assignment of error in this court. The judgment was a general one against the defendants for the amount sued for, and also declaring that same be set and established as a lien on the described property. The court did not err in refusing to amend the judgment and in dismissing the motion of the defendant. "A verdict may be so amended as to make it conform to the pleadings, if the error shall plainly appear upon the face of the record." Code, 110-110. "Judgment and execution shall conform to the verdict." 110-301. The verdict here conformed to the pleadings and was authorized thereby. Consequently, it would have been improper to have sustained the defendants' motion. The judgment followed the verdict. This is not a case, as contended by the defendants, under the Code, 110-112: "If a part of a verdict shall be legal and a part illegal, the court will construe such verdict and order it amended by entering a remitter as to that part which is illegal, and give judgment for the balance." The plaintiff's suit was for a judgment generally against the defendants for lie amount owing it for the lumber sold to the defendants, and therein the plaintiff prayed that such judgment be declared a special lien as to the realty whereon was the building in which the lumber sold the defendants was used. The jury found in favor of the plaintiff generally and in favor of the special lien. The present suit was not in rem only but both in rem and in personam. The plaintiff could seek a general judgment against the defendants and ask for a special lien on the realty improved. See Parish v. Murphy, 51 Ga. 615; Candler v. Partners Loan &c. Co., 96 Ga. 44 (22 S. E. 715); Brown v. Marbutt-Williams Lumber Co., 34 Ga. App. 348, 349 (129 S. E. 575). This was not a suit to foreclose a materialman's lien only. It is not such a case as in Chambers Lumber Co. v. Gilmer, 60 Ga. App. 832, 835 (5 S. E. 2d, 84) or in Spirides v. Victory Lumber Co., 76 Ga. App. 78 (45 S. E. 2d, 65). Haley v. Covington, 19 Ga. App. 782 (92 S. E. 297) is not in point.
The execution was properly levied on any property of the defendants or either of them, and the court did not err in dismissing said motion and in allowing the case to proceed.
Judgments affirmed. Townsend and Carlisle, JJ., concur
Thomas J. Espy Jr., Earl B. Self, contra.
Archibald A. Farrar, for plaintiffs in error.
DECIDED JANUARY 17, 1953.
