Title 29, Chapter 2, Section 81 (29-2-81)

All statements for final settlement with guardians shall be made out on the following basis:

(1) The rate of interest which shall be charged against a guardian on funds held by him without investment and without an order of court authorizing the funds to be held uninvested shall be the same as that provided by law; provided, however, that this Code section shall not be construed to authorize the guardian to use any part of the funds or estate of his ward for his own use or benefit;

(2) No interest shall be charged in favor of or against any party for the first year, as one year is allowed by law for the collection of assets and to ascertain the indebtedness of an estate;

(3) A guardian, in the management of an estate, may retain in his hands at the beginning of each year an amount of money sufficient to pay the current expenses of the year, upon which amount no interest shall be charged in final settlement;

(4) The guardian shall pay interest upon all balances left in his hands at the beginning of each year, over and above the payment of expenses for the year, the same to be ascertained and computed in final settlement;

(5) When, at the beginning of any year, a guardian does not have an amount in hand sufficient to pay the current expenses of the year and his annual returns show that fact, he shall be allowed to charge, in final settlement, interest on the amount advanced by him during the year for such expenses;

(6) The annual returns of guardians shall be made out in the manner regulated by law and practiced in this state. The reservations, charges, and computations of interest set forth in this Code section shall be made when the parties make their statement for final settlement; and

(7) The expenditures and receipts of each year shall be ascertained by reference to annual returns and in all cases the commission annually shall be considered as a part of the annual expenses.
